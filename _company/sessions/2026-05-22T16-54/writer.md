Fellow professionals, I wanted to flag a structural issue that is fundamentally overlooked by most internal compliance teams—and it’s far more damaging than simple 'non-adherence' fines.

We are all familiar with the A-B-C workflow: Data Intake $\rightarrow$ Processing $\rightarrow$ Completion. Our current compliance tools focus on checking if A, B, and C met specific local regulations (e.g., GDPR Article X, CCPA Clause Y). This is merely **reactive auditing**. [근거: 시스템은 아무 문제 없이 작동했지만...]

The real danger lies in the gap between these steps—the crucial 'D Check'. The D Check isn't a single regulation; it’s the structural integrity point where *human judgment* must bridge disparate legal frameworks (e.g., which country's law governs the data residency when processing occurs through three different jurisdictions?). [근거: Pain Point #2]

When your system relies on human interpretation or manual reconciliation at this stage, you are introducing two catastrophic variables:
1. **Human Error:** A missed dependency clause can lead to immediate legal jeopardy.
2. **Conflict of Laws Exposure:** Simply complying with Jurisdiction X might automatically violate the data sovereignty laws of Jurisdiction Y, leading to a massive fine and potential *nullification* of your entire service flow in that region. [근거: 시스템은 아무 문제 없이 작동했지만... 실제 법적 공방에서...]

We need an **'Ex-Ante Compliance Gateway'**—a system that doesn't just check current rules, but models the legal risk landscape across multiple jurisdictions *before* a single line of code is executed or a decision is logged.

If your firm can’t quantify the potential financial loss ($) and time delay (TDR) caused by this 'D Check' gap, you are operating blind. Has anyone successfully implemented a proactive architecture to handle **Cross-Border Regulatory Interoperability**? We need to move beyond mere checklists.
